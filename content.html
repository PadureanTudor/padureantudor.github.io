The Children's Hospital

Built by the people

The Dăruiește Viață Association built Romania's first pediatric oncology hospital through an unprecedented effort driven by civil society, rather than the state. Founded by Carmen Uscatu and Oana Gheorghiu, the organization set out to address the severe shortcomings in the country's medical infrastructure for children with cancer.

During the process, over 350.000 people and 8.000 companies came together to help. This grassroots support was complemented by partnerships with private firms that provided materials, expertise, and pro bono services. In total, the project raised tens of millions of euros without direct government funding, demonstrating the power of collective civic action.

The hospital itself was carefully planned to reflect modern medical practices and the psychological needs of young patients. It includes advanced operating rooms, intensive care units, and specialized oncology wards, as well as spaces designed to reduce stress for children and their families. Bright colors, natural light, and family-friendly areas contrast sharply with the outdated, overcrowded facilities that previously defined pediatric care in Romania.

Impact

The impact of the project has been both immediate and far-reaching. On a practical level, the hospital significantly improves treatment conditions for children with serious illnesses, offering modern operating rooms, advanced intensive care units, and specialized oncology departments. Patients now benefit from safer environments that reduce the risk of infections and support better recovery outcomes.

The hospital has been operational since April 2024, and in it's first year, over 3.500 children have already received treatment there.

Beyond healthcare, the initiative has reshaped public attitudes in Romania. It demonstrated that large-scale, high-quality infrastructure projects can be achieved through civic engagement, transparency, and persistence. The success of Dăruiește Viață has increased pressure on public institutions to improve standards and accountability, while also inspiring other non-governmental organizations and citizen-led movements.

A concrete case

All the numbers so far have been impressive, but it's impossible the grasp the impact this project without talking about a concrete case. Let's talk about Andreea, a little girl aged two and a half. She was actually the first patient transferred into the new hospital when it became operational in April 2024.

Before the move, she had been hospitalized in the old neurosurgery ward of the Marie Curie Children's Hospital, where conditions were extremely cramped, described as being about the size of a small apartment. When she arrived in the new building, the difference was immediate: within a short time, she was already able to play with another child, something that hadn't been possible before in the overcrowded environment.

Other Projects

Other than the hospital, the Association has already achieved many other things, such as:

  • Given equipment to dozens of hospitals across the country
  • Built 20 sterile rooms for the purpose of organ transplantation
  • Renovated oncology sections of hospitals in Bucharest, Timișoara, Brașov and Cluj
  • Helped pass legislation that allowed pacients to get better treatment in other coutries
